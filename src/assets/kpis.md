1. Percentage of people who have been screened and detected early
This measures what percentage of people are identified early with hypertension or diabetes through community or facility-based screening, often before complications appear.

For hypertension: people with elevated blood pressure detected through repeated measurements

For diabetes: people with high blood sugar identified through screening or confirmatory tests

Why we measure: Early detection allows treatment to start sooner, reducing complications, hospitalizations, and costs. Preventative care plus early detection are key. 


2. Percentage of people linked to care
This measures how many people who were screened and identified are successfully connected to a health facility or provider for confirmation, treatment initiation, and follow-up.

Includes referrals completed and first clinical visit attended

Shows whether screening leads to real access to care, not just diagnosis

Why we measure: Screening alone has little impact if people are not connected to treatment and ongoing care.


3. Percentage of diagnosed people with stabilized conditions
This measures how many patients on treatment have their condition under control over time.

For hypertension: blood pressure maintained within controlled ranges

For diabetes: blood glucose levels stabilized according to clinical guidelines

Why we measure: This is the strongest indicator of health impact, showing that people are not only diagnosed and treated, but are living healthier lives with reduced risk of complications.
